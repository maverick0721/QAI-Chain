\section{Introduction}

Off-the-shelf RL loves a stationary room. Governance telemetry rarely cooperates: means stay polite while the policy does brittle things the moment the regime flips or someone poisons a sensor stream. Nobody deletes every handwritten rule either, so the design question we kept circling is how to bolt learning onto deterministic safeguards instead of pretending the safeguards vanished.

QAI-Chain is that bolt-on story. PPO proposes; a cheap uncertainty read says trust or punt to a heuristic; a second deterministic layer---the shield---clips rates and bounds before execution. ``Quantum-inspired'' is a budget label here: a compact VQC-style readout simulated classically gives a nonlinear routing signal without dragging an ensemble into the loop. Hardest intensity we tested: fallback precision 0.964 versus 0.743 for MC-dropout at matched trigger rate (intensity~1.0).

No quantum speedup pitch, no new universal objective. Under fast switching, calibrated routing plus a hard shield reshapes \emph{how} failures arrive compared with policy-only paths, and the uncertainty block stays small.

Empirics stay split. SafetyPointGoal1-v0 and SafetyHalfCheetahVelocity-v1 at thirty seeds each tell you the OmniSafe path is not a toy fork. Governance simulators hurt more: fifty seeds for the controller comparison grid plus sweeps in the appendix. Heuristics stay in every plot---they are not strawmen; in mild cells they still cash checks.

Training delta versus vanilla PPO is modest: variance-aware reward shaping plus a routing rule with threshold $\tau$ and risk weight $\lambda_{\mathrm{risk}}$. Complexity sweeps freeze the backbone and simulator; only those scalars move so we do not confuse ``more safety glue'' with ``different network.''

On-chain commitments are optional receipts after actions land. Useful when two orgs fight over who turned a knob; irrelevant to the control math in the headline tables.

\paragraph{Contributions.}
What we think clears the bar:
\begin{itemize}
    \item Decision-time wiring that couples uncertainty-triggered fallback to deterministic shielding before actuators move.
    \item A deliberately small VQC-style head that still buys nonlinear routing signal when disturbances spike (classical simulation only).
    \item Finished thirty-seed Safety Gym evidence for the two tasks named above, the fifty-seed governance controller suite, and the appendix stress material---all from the same gate/shield code path.
\end{itemize}

\section{Related Perspectives}

Chain+ML hybrids usually live where statistics behave: fraud scores, mempool dashboards, mechanism tweaks. Messy governance telemetry with adversarial seasoning gets slide-deck love long before it gets reproducible benchmarks. Consensus still explains why anyone trusts a shared append-only log \cite{nakamoto2008bitcoin}; PPO is still the boring default for continuous control \cite{schulman2017ppo}. Neither buys you a free pass on testing the loop.

Safe RL already ships CVaR flavors \cite{chow2015cvar}, robust DP lenses \cite{iyengar2005robust}, constrained policy tricks \cite{achiam2017cpo}, plus a zoo of shift hacks. We left the optimizer alone and obsessed over the moment \emph{after} logits exist---routing, shaping, shielding---all under one disturbance recipe so rows stay comparable. Framing is systems work: plumbing plus measurement, not a fresh surrogate loss name.

Compared with the newer risk-sensitive and uncertainty-heavy lines, our delta is mostly architectural---how branch-level routing and a post-check shield sit in a reproducible pipeline. We are not pretending the baseline zoo is complete through 2025; widening that net is future sweat, stated now so nobody expects a survey paper.

Checklist prose and command dumps sit in appendix-style sections; the main thread stays on what actually happened in the runs.
