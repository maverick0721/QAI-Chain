\section{Conclusion}

The primary contribution of this work is parameter-efficient safe RL for non-stationary decentralized settings: uncertainty-gated action routing plus deterministic shielding with a compact estimator. Across stress and uncertainty-focused evaluations, this design preserves safety behavior while materially reducing model size.

Compact VQC modeling in this paper functions as the efficiency mechanism inside the safe RL stack, and blockchain commitments function as a deployment audit feature for multi-party governance. This division of roles clarifies the central claim: gating and shielding provide safety control, compact architecture delivers parameter efficiency, and verifiable logging provides tamper-evident auditability.

The expanded evidence package now includes stronger constrained baselines, completed dual-environment 30-seed transfer evidence, calibration diagnostics, perturbation robustness analysis, and deployment-facing cost appendices. These artifacts can be regenerated through the provided one-command pipeline.
