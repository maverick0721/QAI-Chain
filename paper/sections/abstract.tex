\begin{abstract}
Drift does not always show up first in mean reward. Often the curve still looks polite while the controller is already juddering---noisy value estimates plus twitchy actions---which hurts if you care about bursts, attacks, or latency spikes instead of averaging over friendly weeks.

The stack we ship is a thin PPO-sidecar that only wakes at decision time. One uncertainty scalar routes between the learned action and a fixed heuristic; a shield downstream clips or rejects before the plant sees anything. Beating every baseline on return is out of scope here: we care how the safety--stability trade moves once you stop executing the policy raw. A classically simulated six-qubit-style readout supplies the routing score without ensemble-sized memory.

Evidence is split on purpose: thirty-seed Safety Gym runs for constrained sanity (task IDs in setup), fifty-seed governance controller grids for stress, plus appendix sweeps. One ablation row is ugly on purpose---random wins scalar reward against the full stack---because reward alone can lie and we care about violations and tracking when the schedule shifts.

Numbers in the tables trace to version-controlled artifacts; the reproducibility section lists how to regenerate them from the repository.
\end{abstract}
