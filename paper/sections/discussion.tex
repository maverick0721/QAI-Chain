\section{Discussion}

Skim-level takeaway: we are not pitching a universally stronger learner. We are pitching a thin decision-time wrapper that changes \emph{how} failures unfold once the world slides under the policy. Treat every figure as a trade plot---where raw policy execution goes brittle, gate plus hard shield often buys constraint slack by spending return.

Safety Gym rows exist so you know we are not only storytelling on bespoke sims. Thirty seeds each, same protocol block as elsewhere. Governance sims are where regime switches and calibration drift actually show up in ink.

Random stays in the table even when it wins reward. Reward shaping games exist; ``top of the leaderboard'' is a lousy safety proof. The gated stack sometimes picks conservative, shield-shaped moves that reward engineers hate even while violation and tracking curves look saner.

Payoff shows up when switching intensity climbs. Calibrated uncertainty answers ``when to fall back'' without renting an ensemble, and the shield caps tail risk when the gate guesses wrong. That is why the tiny head is worth mentioning at all: you can ship it beside the controller.

This is additive to modern safe-RL objectives, not a replacement. OmniSafe rows are a reminder that return order reshuffles with budget and environment while safety cost behaves like a frontier.

\subsection{Why Uncertainty-Gated Control Works}

Three mechanisms, one caveat: they talk to each other.

Gating is branch picking under shift---when epistemic noise spikes, handing off to a heuristic avoids committing to policy logits in the window where policy-only paths wobble hardest.

Shielding is not the same job. Even if the gate picks wrong, rate caps and action boxes still trim the trajectories that usually show up in incident writeups.

Risk-shaped training starves the cheap cheat where the policy chases short reward spikes that fight stable control intent. No fairy dust---just one fewer cheat code against the scalar.

Under disturbance those pieces line up because the stack targets failure containment, not squeezing a few more return points in an easy stationary room.

\subsection{Failure Cases}

Late fallback when calibration sprints ahead of the gate shows up as a short violation burst. Disturbances outside the shield vocabulary make the policy and heuristic chase each other and tracking wobbles.

In our logs those episodes end; they still argue for tighter online calibration and shields that encode more mismatch. Qualitatively that tracks Corollary~2---bounded observation noise keeps regret growing about linearly instead of cliff-diving.

Toy physics, partly post-hoc uncertainty scaling, thin baseline net versus what production sign-off wants, richer economic adversaries missing: all true. Live governance needs state, adversaries, and monitors we did not build.

If someone extends this: swap variance hacks for real calibrated heads, stack explicit hard constraints on top of routing, train on one disturbance atlas and test on another without a full retune.

Shipping advice stays boring: keep deterministic fallback, gate on uncertainty, plot safety next to reward \cite{pineau2021improving,dror2018hitchhikers}---leaderboards alone will gaslight you.
