\section{System Design}

The main system claim is a safe RL control loop with compact uncertainty estimation, while blockchain serves as a deployment-time audit substrate. For clarity, the online decision path is:
\[
\text{state telemetry} \rightarrow \text{policy proposal} \rightarrow \text{uncertainty gate} \rightarrow \text{shield} \rightarrow \text{executed action}.
\]

Audit records are committed to chain as a verifiable log after execution. This division keeps the core method in the RL/safety domain while preserving multi-party provenance when control actions are reviewed by mutually untrusted participants.

\subsection{Data and Control Flow}

Transaction and peer events update chain state through RPC and validation logic. Governance telemetry is encoded into a compact vector, and the controller emits bounded actions for difficulty, block size, and gas limit:
\begin{equation}
\begin{aligned}
\text{State telemetry} &\rightarrow \text{policy proposal} \rightarrow \text{uncertainty gate} \\
&\rightarrow \text{safety shield} \rightarrow \text{on-chain commit}.
\end{aligned}
\end{equation}

The uncertainty gate can route to deterministic fallback before execution; the shield can reject or clamp invalid actions. This paper's performance claims are tied to that gated-and-shielded control path. Extended blockchain architecture detail is provided in the appendix.

\subsection{API Contracts}

RPC endpoints use typed request/response schemas. Invalid payloads are rejected and negative-path contract tests cover malformed or missing fields.

Contract tests are the boundary between protocol logic and adaptive governance logic.

\subsection{Operational Guardrails}

Deployment reliability depends on typed interfaces, contract-tested RPC boundaries, and deterministic control handoff between policy, gate, and shield. These controls reduce interface-level failure modes and keep execution behavior auditable across nodes.
