\section{Limitations}

Governance simulators stay toy-scale on purpose---no realistic peer churn, no endogenous liquidity games, no mempool microstructure worth the name. The VQC-style block and audit hooks are lab prototypes, not hardened services. Latency came from one workstation profile, not a GPU sweep menu.

Reward shaping can reorder winners (random beating the full stack is the blunt example), so leaning on scalar return alone is silly. Some slices outside the fifty-seed constrained suite still run thinner seed counts. Simulator fidelity caps how far uncertainty and network physics claims travel, and we do not yet model nasty economic adversaries inside mining or tx flows. Baseline coverage stops well short of a 2022--2025 safe-RL catalog.

Corollary~2 needs bounded perturbations and clean assumptions; it is not a shield against arbitrary sensor pathology.

Safety Gym gives standard constrained-control cover; it does not license plugging this straight into a live chain without the gaps above getting closed.
